The motherboard of a computer is like a tree trunk; there are many branches connected to it. The main purpose of a motherboard is to help the different parts of the computer communicate with each other. Without the motherboard, even the CPU cannot operate.

We’ll cover several major components of the motherboard:

Sockets

Most motherboards have a CPU socket which restricts the type of processor that can be connected to it. This restriction is enforced with the use of a Pin Grid Array (PGA). The PGA works similar to a shape sorter toy for children. The processor can only fit on the socket if the holes in the socket perfectly match the pins on the processor.

Some newer CPU models do not have a PGA. Instead, they have a Land Grid Array (LGA). The LGA contains the pins directly on the socket instead of the processor. The processor is then mounted on the socket and secured in place.

The other components of the motherboard communicate with the CPU through the chipset.

Chipset

As mentioned above, the chipset handles the communication between the CPU, memory and the Accelerated Graphics Port (AGP, also known as the video card). The chipset consists of a ‘set of chips’ called the northbridge and the southbridge. These two chips have different functions as outlined below:

Northbridge

The northbridge is called that because it is usually located above the southbridge on the motherboard. The northbridge and southbridge are literally ‘bridges’ between the major motherboard components.
